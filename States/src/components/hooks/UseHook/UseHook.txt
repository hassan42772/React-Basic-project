UseHook is better than UseContextApi because it allows you to manage state and side effects in a more modular and reusable way. With UseHook, you can create custom hooks that encapsulate specific logic and can be easily shared across different components. This promotes code reusability and separation of concerns, making your codebase cleaner and easier to maintain. On the other hand, UseContextApi is primarily used for managing global state and may not be as flexible for handling complex logic or side effects. Additionally, UseHook allows you to take advantage of React's built-in hooks like useState and useEffect, which can further enhance the functionality of your custom hooks. Overall, UseHook provides a more powerful and flexible way to manage state and side effects in your React applications compared to UseContextApi.
